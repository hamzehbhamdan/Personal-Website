About Me

Hi! I’m Hamzeh, a student at Harvard College studying Applied Mathematics with a focus in Economics and am a concurrent masters candidate in Computational Science and Engineering.

My academic passions lie in the intersections between mathematics, economics, and computer science. Through my work, I try to understand and model why we do the things we do and how we can do them better - from computational economic modeling to quantitative statistical analyses.

Throughout my past semesters at Harvard, I’ve helped plan an international conference, consulted for several Fortune 50 companies, led a team of 35 students to source $565k in consulting cases for Harvard Undergraduate Consulting on Business and the Environment, conducted research with Harvard Undergraduate Foreign Policy Initiative for NATO, and am currently in the process of learning different forms of data analyses.

One thing I’ve noticed at Harvard is that, for many, there is a large barrier to acquiring knowledge. To some, it is monetary, and to others, it is complexity. As part of my passion for education, I am working on an ongoing project on making knowledge more accessible; for now, this will include: college applications, computer science, game theory, and microeconomic modeling.
